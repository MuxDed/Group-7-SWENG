Clare Brophy's Art Gallery

Athlone born, Clare Brophy has her late grandfather to thank for her early introduction to Art. During sojourns on his farm near Ballinasloe, he taught her to observe the world around her, how seasonal changed impacted the natural environment, the wildlife and the animals he reared. He was a craftsman who encouraged her to use her hands, so while he was busy in his workshop, she would make mud cakes and mini mud sculptures. Childhood memories of the bog lands rich with its hues of purples heathers, muted natural tones coupled with tactile experiences of textures such as bog cotton and báinín (undyed wool) have left her with lifelong grá for nature which proliferated her work.
